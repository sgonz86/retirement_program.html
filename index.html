A Career of Service

Senior Chief Gonzalez, a proud New Jersey native from Old Bridge, began his Navy journey in August 2005. After his initial training, he was assigned to Strike Fighter Squadron 31 (VF-31), where he became a plane captain. He holds a special place in Navy history as part of the final F-14D Tomcat maintenance crew.

In 2007, Senior Chief Gonzalez transitioned to Master-At-Arms and headed to his first overseas duty station in Bahrain. There, he was entrusted with the critical role of Coxswain, piloting Harbor Security Vessels to protect the 5th Fleet. This achievement earned him the prestigious Small Craft Insignia device. His next tour took him to Greece, where he took on numerous responsibilities as a patrolman, Watch Commander, and weapons armorer. His leadership was recognized in 2010 when he was promoted to E-5 and named Sailor of the Year.

His career truly took off at Maritime Expeditionary Security Squadron 4 in Virginia. As a Mission Commander and team member for specialized units, he completed three deployments, navigating diverse and complex environments. He was promoted to E-6 and led his platoon as the Leading Petty Officer.

In 2014, Senior Chief Gonzalez's dedication led him to Naval Expeditionary Intelligence Command in Virginia Beach, where he served as Command Master-At-Arms. His hard work was honored with the Senior Sailor of the Year award in 2016 and a promotion to Chief Petty Officer. His next assignment took him to San Diego, California, aboard the USS LAKE CHAMPLAIN (CG-57). As the Command Chief Master-At-Arms, he was a key legal advisor to the ship's leadership during a deployment that spanned the Pacific.

His final overseas tour was in Rota, Spain, where he was the Physical Security Leading Chief Petty Officer. Promoted to E-8, he was later named the Anti-terrorism and Physical Security Leading Chief Petty Officer. Senior Chief Gonzalez concluded his distinguished career back home in New Jersey, serving as the Senior Enlisted Leader for Naval Security Forces at Naval Weapons Station Earle.

A graduate of the U.S. Navy Senior Enlisted Academy, Senior Chief Gonzalez earned a Bachelor of Science in Criminal Justice. He holds several prestigious warfare qualifications, including Enlisted Expeditionary Warfare Specialist and Enlisted Aviation Warfare Specialist. His service has been recognized with multiple awards, including the Navy and Marine Corps Commendation Medal (five awards) and the Navy and Marine Corps Achievement Medal (six awards). He plans to retire with his wife and their puppy, Frank Sinatra, and pursue his passion for real estate in New Jersey.
